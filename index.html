Impressionism!!!

History

Impressionism came about in the late 1800s; it was a radical art movement that rejected the traditional, realistic style of the time. On the contrary, it is actually coined the first modern movement in painting. Impressionist art drew inspiration from a Japanese style, ukiyo-e.

Characteristics

Impressionism is characterized by thin, small, visible brush strokes. Artists often utilize layering (without blending) in their painting to add dimension. Impressionist art does not pay attention to detail; it focuses on the piece as whole. Artists use complimentary colors to depict shadows (not black and grey), which creates a brighter painting overall. Lastly, impressionist artists paint outdoors, not in a studio. This allows them to better grasp the beauty of their surroundings.

Notable Artists

Claude Monet, Paul Cezanne, Alfred Sisley

Famous Works
